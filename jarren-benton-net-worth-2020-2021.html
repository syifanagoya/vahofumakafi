Career

Benton’s musical career took off in the year 2009 when he released his debut mixtape, Jarren Benton: The Mixtape. The album was produced by the Beatgods. Further, in 2011, he released a mixtape titled, ‘Huffing Glue With Hasselhoff.’ This project was in collaboration with the producer SMKA.

This was followed by a chain of events that transformed Benton’s life. The video reached the CEO of Funk Volume, an independent record label in America. Hopsin, an American underground rapper, was the one who forwarded the video to the CEO of Funk Volume, Dame Ritter.

This eventually led Ritter and Hopsin signing Benton up to the label in May 2012. Benton soon started receiving many offers for signing with top labels and collaborations with leading artists. The first release with Funk Volume was ‘Freebasing with Kevin Bacon,’ which was a mixtape featuring Rittz, Freeway, Jon Connor, and other artists.

2013 was a dream come true for Benton. He released his first-ever studio album titled, My Grandma’s Basement. It had guest appearances from many leading artists. He would later find himself touring the country for the same album, ‘Grandma’s Basement Tour.’

Benton’s second studio album was released in 2016, which was produced by his own label called Benton Enterprises. A year later, he released his third studio album, ‘The Mink Coat Killa LP.” He has since then worked on a series of collaborations with other artists.
